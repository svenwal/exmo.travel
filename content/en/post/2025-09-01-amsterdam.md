## Amsterdam

After all the beautiful, peaceful nature, it’s time for a contrast — off to the big city of Amsterdam.  
We found a campsite close to the old town (just one free(!) ferry ride from the [Central Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam_Centraal_station)) and surprisingly stood surrounded by greenery at [Camping Vliegenbos](https://www.amsterdam.nl/vliegenbos/).

![Amsterdam Centraal](/images/blog/2025-09-Amsterdam/Amsterdam_Central_Station.jpeg)
[Amsterdam Centraal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam_Centraal_station)

![View of the Amstel from Central Station](/images/blog/2025-09-Amsterdam/Aussicht_auf_Amstel.jpeg)
View of the [Amstel River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amstel) from [Central Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam_Centraal_station)

Day one in the city was all about the typical tourist spots — we started at the [Central Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam_Centraal_station) and wandered through the old town and the shopping street — past the kind of shops you find in every city, but also countless stores selling lollies, cookies, and other treats infused with rather *interesting* special ingredients. That’s really omnipresent here — at every corner.

At the [Munttoren](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Munttoren), there are more and more lovely views of the [canals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canals_of_Amsterdam) — simply beautiful.

Day two was inspired by the [Reisen Reisen Podcast](https://www.reisen-reisen-der-podcast.de/amsterdam-noord/) (a recommendation from Marco), and we stayed on our side of the Amstel (by the way, that’s where the city’s name comes from — dam on the Amstel) and explored the trendy areas of [Amsterdam-Noord](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam-Noord).  
There you’ll find old industrial and shipyard sites that now house the creative scene — up to and including a [hotel in a crane](https://www.stern.de/reise/service/ratgeber-hotels/crane-hotel-faralda-amsterdam_7284928-7284804.html).

## De Biesbosch

After so much big city, we needed a quick reset — so on our way toward Rotterdam, we made a short stop at [De Biesbosch National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_Biesbosch_National_Park) and hiked for several hours between water and forest. A beautiful loop and yet another proof that where there’s a will, there’s a parking spot for us (or two in a row ;))

## New Tires

With about 70,000 km on the clock, our Mammothon is still “practically new,” but the rear tires have reached their end. One of them actually completely — it had been flat for the previous owners, so the empty rim had just been hanging on the lift. That’s now fixed, and brand-new (and expensive) [395/80R20 Michelin XZL2](https://business.michelin.de/reifen/michelin-x-force-2-xzl2) tires are now on the rear axle, and the former spare wheel is back in its proper place.

![New tires](/images/blog/2025-09-Amsterdam/Neue_Reifen.jpeg)
New tires

![Lou helps](/images/blog/2025-09-Amsterdam/Lou_beim_Reifenwechsel.jpeg)
Lou helping with the tire change

It’ll definitely be interesting if I ever have to change a wheel off-road. Not so much the swap itself (hard enough already...) but rather getting the other tire back up onto the lift. I’ve upgraded that a bit now — not with a winch, but at least some tie-down straps that make it easier. They also serve as a secondary safety feature.
